10 Best Beach Movies To Watch To Get Into Vacation Mode

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, June 24, 2024

While seeing a beach-themed movie can make you worry that you’ll need a bigger boat, these movies are carefully chosen to capture the spirit of a beach getaway and entice us to jump back in the sea and ride off into the distance. The most comfortable aspect of it is that you can enjoy all of these from the comfort of your couch without the need for summer body. 50 First Dates

Adam Sandler has frequently used Hawaii as the backdrop for his romantic comedies since it is such a breathtakingly gorgeous location. Unusually, Henry (Sandler) falls for local artist Lucy (Drew Barrymore), but he soon learns that she suffers from a unique form of amnesia that makes her forget him every time she goes to sleep. With every new day, he tries to charm her once more. The plot makes the most of its gorgeous background by having a lot of the action take place outside. For example, when Henry plans ways to stop Lucy on the road, the rolling hills are displayed. Lucy’s important amnesia revelation scene is even placed at the end of a long pier. The movie makes sure that the Hawaiian setting will be enduring.

Blue Hawaii

Blue Hawaii offers all the beach vacation amenities you could possibly need, including romance, music, whimsy, sun, surf, and sandy beaches. Elvis’ vacation was enjoyed by the audience, and the movie was so well-liked that many of his later productions used the same formula (putting Elvis in an exotic setting, then heaping on romance and songs till the joyful ending), but none of them were as successful as Blue Hawaii. Blue Lagoon

Two young children (Brooke Shields and Christopher Atkins) are abandoned on a lush tropical island when their ship sinks. The kids are forced to deal with surviving the island, a nearby cannibal clan, and their own developing emotional and sexual relationships for one another as the years go by. Even though the movie received poor reviews, it was a great commercial hit and one of the highest-grossing movies of 1980. Just Go With It

A dishonest boss and a youngster who won’t bargain are all you need to get an all-expenses paid trip to Hawaii. In the surprisingly funny Just Go With It, Katherine (Jennifer Aniston) and Danny (Adam Sandler) wind up on a lavish Hawaiian vacation. The movie is carefree, lighthearted, and enjoyable like a summer vacation; it is only made all the more alluring by its supporting cast, which includes Nicole Kidman, Brooklyn Decker, and the breathtaking Hawaii scenery. Mamma Mia!

Mamma Mia! uses the hits of ABBA to tell the story of Sophie (Amanda Seyfried) attempting to discover which of the three men (Pierce Brosnan, Stellan Skarsgård, Colin Firth) her mother Donna (Meryl Streep) had a relationship with is her father, in order to have the right man give her away at her upcoming wedding. With its joyful attitude, sun-kissed cheeks, and amazing variety of musical performances, the film is a campy classic that brilliantly utilises the magnificent setting of the Greek Islands. If you’ve ever fantasised of having a destination wedding, this movie will remind you of the heady fizz of a glass of champagne.

The Endless Summer

The Endless Summer, a groundbreaking surfing documentary shot on location all around the world, will make you appreciate how stunning the world can be. They had no idea the wanderlust their film would arouse when filmmaker Bruce Brown, surfers Robert August and Michael Hynson, and they ventured off in 1966 in search of warm seas and the ideal wave. After more than 50 years, viewers are still tempted to give up their regular lives and pursue a life filled with wonder, adventure, sand, surf, and sun.

The Odyssey

The beach offers activities beyond surfing, tanning, and drinking cocktails. The Odyssey, a biopic of renowned French director, researcher, scientist, and diver Jacques Cousteau, will transport you to a subaquatic world. Cousteau was a champion of investigating the depths of the ocean and bringing the dark secrets of the sea into the living rooms of his audience. He was a clear inspiration for filmmakers like Wes Anderson and James Cameron. You will be enticed to take up a scuba mask and explore the undersea world on your own after watching the film because it has some beautiful underwater imagery.

The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants

The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants will make you nostalgic for the days of carefree summer vacations with your buddies. With Tibby (Amber Tamblyn) staying home, Carmen (America Ferrera) visiting South Carolina, Bridget (Blake Lively) visiting Baja California in Mexico, and Lena (Alexis Bledel) taking a plane to the Greek island of Santorini, this movie has the advantage of examining four separate vacations. Santorini is a sea of white and blue houses accentuated with vibrant flowers, surrounded by smooth sand, and beautiful ocean; it is possibly even more wonderful than a pair of jeans that fit four adolescent girls of varying sizes. Even if the locations aren’t your cup of tea, the touching tale of four friends who support one another through all of life’s ups and downs will make you feel good inside.
