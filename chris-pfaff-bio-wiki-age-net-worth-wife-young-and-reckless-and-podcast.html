Chris Pfaff Biography

Chris Pfaff is an American clothing designer, music producer, and reality television star who was born in Akron, Ohio as Christopher Russel Pfaff. Pfaff first starred in Rob & Big, then he went on to play a major role in Rob Dyrdek’s Fantasy Factory, and he also guest-starred in Ridiculousness.

Chris Drama Pfaff is also a DJ, as well as the creator of the streetwear line known as Young & Reckless. He was born and raised in Ohio where he pursued his education from Coventry High School and graduated in 2005. He later moved to Los Angeles to earn a professional skating career, but he ended up becoming Rob’s personal assistant.

Chris Pfaff Clothing Line

Pfaff founded, Young & Reckless offers tee shirts, tank tops, sweatshirts, outerwear, swimwear, and other apparel and accessories for both men and women. He said that when his clothing line became a hit he decided to focus more on it and less on music production.

Chris Pfaff Young and Reckless

Chris is the founder of ‘Young and Reckless’, a streetwear fashion line. He launched the brand when he was 22 years old and had no experience in marketing or branding nor outside capital. He had a friend who knew graphic design, and he sketched as well as designed the original logo.

Six years later after launching, in the year 2015, the brand grew into a nationwide brand, with distribution in more than 3,000 Macy’s, Dillard’s, and PacSun stores. The brand got endorsements from Puff Daddy and Justin Bieber. It had 43 employees and $31 million in revenue annually. Chris said that he made a mistake with a celebrity while trying to promote his cloth line.

“We did this $150,000 campaign with a celebrity when we were in a phase where I believed in myself a little too much, and I believed in the brand a little too much.

I thought you could just grab a celebrity and have them wear your clothes, and it would look nice, and it would work. But the celebrity didn’t fit the brand. There was no story to tell. There was nothing reckless to tell. The campaign wasn’t just a loss, it hurt the brand long term.”
