After leaving the WJZ weather team, what is Chelsea Ingram’s new job?

Chelsea Ingram is an Emmy-nominated meteorologist and environmental scientist who has appeared on CBS Evening News, CBS This Morning, and CBSN throughout the country.

Ingram is a well-known meteorologist who has been in the weather broadcasting field for over a decade, and she is best recognized for her work with CBS Baltimore’s WJZ.

North Carolina State University awarded the meteorologist a Bachelor of Science in Atmospheric Sciences and Meteorology. Chelsea has a Master of Science in Environmental Science and Policy from Johns Hopkins University Advanced Academic Programs, as well.

Chelsea Ingram’s New Job at WeatherWorks in 2022: Senior Meteorologist

Chelsea Ingram, an Emmy-nominated meteorologist and environmental scientist, appears to have joined WBAL-TV, an NBC affiliate in Baltimore, Maryland. She stunned her followers when she predicted the weather for the television station on June 8, 2022. Chelsea Ingram has joined WBAL-TV 11 as a meteorologist.

She also works for WeatherWorks, LLC, a private weather consulting organization focused on providing clients with rapid, dependable, and accurate meteorological services. According to her Linkedin profile, the meteorologist began working with the weather forecasting firm in April of 2022, and she has been there for three months.

Chelsea Ingram’s Departure from the WJZ Weather Team: Why?

Ingram may have departed CBS Baltimore’s WJZ to pursue other chances rather than staying with the same television station. She has not, however, revealed the reason for her departure from the television network.

Although the meteorologist has kept the reason for her departure private, we assume she received offers from other weather forecasting firms; she is a well-known figure with extensive experience in the field.

She joined CBS Philly, the weather, breaking news, traffic, and sports service for Philadelphia, after announcing her departure from WJZ on April 5, 2017, and worked there from April 2013 until April 2017. Chelsea also worked for ViacomCBS before joining WeatherWorks as a senior meteorologist, and she was instrumental in the establishment of WBAL-TV.
