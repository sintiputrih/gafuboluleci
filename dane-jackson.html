Dane Jackson (born 29 November 1996) is an American football cornerback for the Pittsburgh Steelers of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Pittsburgh.

Dane Jackson Early Years : 

Jackson attended East Stroudsburg South High School in East Stroudsburg, Pennsylvania. As a senior, he had 50 tackles and four interceptions. Dane was named to the 2016 Big 33 Football Classic.

Dane Jackson Family:

Jackson is the son of Dion Jackson and Nicole Oliver. Dane has three brothers, Devonte, Dennis Jr., and Donovan. His cousin, Stephon Morris, also played cornerback at Penn State University and in the NFL.

Dane Jackson Career:

The Pittsburgh Steelers drafted Jackson in the seventh round (232nd overall) of the 2020 NFL Draft. He made his NFL debut on September 14, 2020, against the New York Giants. In Week 2, against the Denver Broncos, he recorded his first career interception off a pass thrown by Jeff Driskel.

On January 3, 2021, Jackson was placed on injured reserve.

In the 2020 season, Jackson had 22 tackles and one interception in 16 games.

He had four tackles and one pass in two games in the playoffs.

In the 2021 season, Jackson is expected to play a key role in the Steelers’ secondary.
