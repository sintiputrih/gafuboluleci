How old is Sha'Carri Richardson?

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, January 21, 2024

Sha'Carri Richardson, born on March 25, 2000, in Dallas, Texas, is a 23-year-old American track and field sprinter who has made waves in the athletic world with her remarkable speed and accomplishments. Despite a rough start to life, being raised by her grandmother and aunt after her mother abandoned her, Richardson found solace and passion in sprinting from a young age. Her early achievements in sprinting at the US Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) Junior Olympics in 2016 marked the beginning of her journey to becoming a track and field sensation.

Collegiate and Professional Career

In 2018, Richardson enrolled at Louisiana State University (LSU) and began competing for the LSU Lady Tigers track and field team. She made headlines in 2019 at the NCAA Division I Championships by breaking the collegiate record in the 100-meter sprint with a time of 10.75 seconds and setting a new U20 world record. This stellar performance led her to turn professional in June 2019, signing a contract with Nike and training under former Olympic sprinter Dennis Mitchell.

Notable Achievements and Rise to Prominence

Richardson's career reached new heights in 2023, as she claimed the top spot in the world rankings for the 100m and 200m events. Her exceptional performance at the World Athletics Championships in Budapest in the same year solidified her status as one of the most dominant female sprinters globally. Personal Life and Advocacy

Aside from her athletic prowess, Richardson is known for her unapologetic style, which includes vibrant colors and distinctive hairstyles. She has also been vocal about her identity as a member of the LGBTQ community, openly expressing her bisexuality and acknowledging her girlfriend. Furthermore, Richardson is involved in philanthropic work, contributing to For Oak Cliff, an organization dedicated to supporting disadvantaged youth.

At the age of 23, she has already left an indelible mark in the world of track and field, and her future in the sport looks incredibly promising.
