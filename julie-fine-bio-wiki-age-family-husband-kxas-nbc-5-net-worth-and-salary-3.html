    Julie Fine Career

    Immediately after graduation from the university, Julie moved to Washington, D.C. Here, she was working on WJLA-TV as a production assistant and field producer. She worked as the only local reporter who would accompany students to scholastic competitions happening in Anchorage, Alaska.

    Her debut career in journalism was in 1999 when she joined WJET-TV as an educational reporter. She has had the chance to cover politics and breaking news in Buffalo, New York. Julie covered the resignation of Government Eliot Spitzer.

    Also, she had an interview with Wolf Blitzer and Tim Russert on the Buffalo journalist story. Later on, she was back in Washington to cover Russert’s funeral. This was after graduating from Tulane University. She did news reports until 2002 when she moved to WKBW-TV in 2002.

    While at WKBW-TV, Julie was working as a reporter. This was the longest time she has worked in a station in her journalism career. Fine was in WKBW-TV for 8 years, i.e from 2002 to 2010.

    Julie Fine KXAS – TV

    After moving from WKBW-TV, Julie joined WPXI-TV in the same year – 2010. Here, she was working as a reporter. In June 2013, she joined KXAS, NBC 5 in Dallas, Fort Worth Area.

    As of now, she is working as a political reporter for KXAS, NBC 5 News. While working in the station, she covered the Gubernatorial elections in 2014. Also, she had the chance to cover the 84th and 85th Texas legislative sessions.

    Also, she was present in the long Presidential election in 2016. Julie specifically tackled Senator Ted Cruz’s (R-TX) run for presidency story. Fine was present from Iowa until Indiana where Ted Cruz suspended his political run.

    In between the politics, Julie was lucky to have a one-on-one interview with him. During the campaign, Julie has had the pleasure of being in Iowa, New Hampshire, South Carolina, Florida, and Texas.
