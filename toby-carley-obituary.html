We are deeply saddened to share with you that Toby Carley passed away. Toby Carley’s death deeply affected his friends and family members. They are devastated after hearing the news of his death. In this post, you will get all the information related to his death.

When Was Toby Carley’s Funeral Services Held?

Toby Carley passed away on December 11, 2023, in Grantsburg, Wisconsin. On December 16, 2023, visitation was held at Rowe Funeral Homes in Frederic, WI, from 9:00 AM to 11:00 AM.

The funeral service was followed at 11:00 AM at St. Dominic’s Catholic Church in Frederic, WI. Below we shared a post related to his funeral services.

Toby Carley was more than a resident of Grantsburg; he was a friend, a neighbor and a pillar of support for those who knew him. Toby’s warm personality created a ripple effect, spreading joy wherever he went.

After Toby’s death Carolyn ‘Kothbauer’ Peterson posted on Facebook, “RIP Toby Carley. Friendships run deep after all those years of teaching together. I’ll miss you.”

What Work Did Toby Carley Do in His Life?

Of all his loved ones, Toby loved his girls the most. He was hard-working, always friendly and smiling and seemed to know everyone. Toby was born on January 28, 1974, in St. Croix Falls. His parents are Jeff and Pat Carley.

Toby attended Frederic High School and went on to study at UW River Falls and UW LaCrosse. He worked at ABC Lumber, Northwoods Lumber, Burnett Dairy, Anderson Construction, Luck School District and Burnett Dairy. He enjoyed working at Anderson Construction throughout the summers while he was a teacher and a high school and college student.

He learnt a lot about construction there. He had a fulfilling 23-year career as an athletic director, football and wrestling coach and teacher in the Luck School District. Following that, he worked for Grantsburg’s Burnett Dairy and ABC Lumber, Webster’s Northwoods Lumber and, most recently, as a solo contractor, doing whatever was required of him.

Toby took great pride in his voluntary work with the fire departments of both Frederic and Grantsburg, where he had previously lived. Hunting, fishing, camping and canoeing were Toby’s favorite outdoor activities.
