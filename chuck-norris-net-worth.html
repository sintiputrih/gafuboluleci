Chuck Norris has an incredible net worth of $70 million.

He is an American actor, martial arts artist, screenwriter, and producer.

Chuck Norris, originally Carlos Ray Norris, was born on March 10, 1940, in Oklahoma.

The American actor is renowned for his martial arts and badass attitude in his movies.

He’s a black belt in Tang Soo Do, Brazilian jiu-jitsu, and judo, even owns a martial arts academy called Chun Kuk Do.

He won the middleweight karate champion title in 1968 and held it for six years in a row.

In 1969, he got the most victories in the Oklahoma Martial Art tournament and was named Triple Crown of Martial Arts.

Moreover, He joined the US Air Force of United States as an Air Force Police.

Furthermore, he was sent to Osan Air Base in South Korea and started his training at Tang Soo Do.

 In 1958,  at Osan Air Base in South Korea, he was nicknamed as Chuck.

After returning from the U.S.  Army service, he trained martial arts to  Hollywood celebrities.

Eventually, he started working on action films and started his new career in Hollywood.

Charity

Chuck Norris is famous for making contributions to different charity organizations.

Some of the organizations he’s made his contributions are Funds for Kids, the United Way,  the Make-A-Wish Foundation, and Veteran’s Administration National Salute to Hospitalized Veterans.

Further, he is the founder of the United Fighting Arts Federation and Kickstart Kids.

Both organizations were established in 1990, with the goal to grow discipline and focus in children as a tactic to keep them away from drugs by training them in martial arts.

In 2005, Norris introduced the World Combat League (WCL), a martial arts competition, of which part of the revenue is separated for his Kickstart Kids program.

Moreover, he has donated to support Paediatric HIV/AIDS homes in Delhi, a blind school in Karnataka, and mentally ill patients and HIV/AIDS infected adults.

Furthermore, he has always supported Republican Party and donated over $32,000 for Republican candidates and parties during U.S. elections since 1988.
