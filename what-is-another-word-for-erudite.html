What is another word for erudite

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, February 8, 2024

What is the meaning of erudite person?

Definition of erudite

: having or showing knowledge that is gained by studying : possessing or displaying erudition an erudite scholar.

Is erudite an insult?

The root of the word “erudite” is the Latin “rudis,” meaning “rude” (or untrained, unskilled, rough). The Latin “erudire” meant “to train, instruct,” so someone who is erudite is no longer rude, but has been well-instructed, well-taught.

What is an example of erudite?

The definition of erudite is someone who has wide range of knowledge and is well-read. An example of erudite is a professor of literature. Having or showing a wide knowledge gained from reading; learned; scholarly.

Is erudite a compliment?

That is a compliment! Being erudite means that you have or show a wide knowledge gained from reading; learned; scholarly. Readers cannot fail to recognize Dante’s erudition. He appears to have taken all learning for his province, or what passed for learning then.

What is the plural form of erudite?

erudite (plural erudites) a learned or scholarly person.

What is the noun form of erudite?

erudition. Profound knowledge, especially that based on learning and scholarship.

Where is erudite?

Millennium Park The Erudite headquarters is located somewhere near, or in, Chicago’s Millennium Park, as stated in the first book when Tris describes a large rusted lima bean sculpture near the compound, which is most likely the famous “Bean” sculpture in the park.

Can erudite be used as an adjective?

Erudition comes from the adjective erudite, which describes something that is full of knowledge or that is scholarly.

Are the erudite evil?

Erudite isn’t evil, but they had been boiling for far too long with far too much power and it did boil over.

What is the government called in divergent?

Abnegation In Divergent, Abnegation is the governing body and no other factions are allowed to take part in controlling the government. Capitalist ideals are represented in the Erudite faction.

What is erudite in divergent?

In the Divergent book trilogy and film series, factions are societal divisions that classify citizens based on their aptitudes and values. The factions are Dauntless (the brave), Amity (the kind), Erudite (the intelligent), Abnegation (the selfless), and Candor (the honest).

Why are abnegation called stiff?

The Abnegations are called ‘Stiffs’ because married couples only touch hands in front of others. They don’t hug each other, so whenever someone tries to hug them, they become stiff.

What are the 4 factions in divergent?

The factions are called Abnegation (selfless), Erudite (intellectual), Dauntless (brave), Candor (honest), and Amity (peaceful). Beatrice, the main character, was born into Abnegation, but she’s far from selfless.

Why does abnegation hate erudite?

Erudite harbor a fierce grudge against Abnegation, believing that they are secret-keepers who are not nearly as selfless as they seem. They seek power over the government, and on many occasions sabotage members of Abnegation to get what they want. They also use Dauntless as their soldiers near the end of Divergent.

Why do dauntless wear black?

Dauntless are those who are brave, seeming almost fearless. … Members of Dauntless dress in tight-fitting, dark or black clothing that aids in their lifestyle of battle and training. Dark makeup, tattoos, and piercings are also recommended for all members. To get to their base, they jump on and off a train every day.

Is Tris’s mother divergent?

In fact, just like Tris, Natalie Prior is Divergent (35.37). So Tris spent all this time beating herself up for not being like her mom—when, in fact, she’s exactly like her mom, in more ways than she ever imagined.

What’s the difference between Amity and abnegation?

Abnegation are selfless to a fault, they think about helping others and not being a burden. Amity is about kindness, but it’s more about forgiveness and peace.
