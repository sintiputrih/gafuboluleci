Erin Moran Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, May 26, 2024

Who was Erin Moran?

Erin Moran was an American actress. She is best known for her role as Joanie Cunningham on the television sitcom ‘Happy Days and in its spin-off ‘Joanie Loves Chachi’.

Who are the parents of Erin Moran?

Erin Moran was born in Burbank California on October 18, 1960. Her birth name is Erin Marie Moran-Fleishmann and she was raised in nearby North Hollywood.

Her father’s name was Edward Moran and her mother’s name was Sharon Moran. Moran’s father was a finance manager and she was the second youngest of six children.

She was supported by her mother who signed her with an agent when she was five years old. Her brothers are John Moran and Tony Moran, and both are actors.

Tony played Michael Myres during a scene in ‘Halloween’ in 1978. His sister’s name is Kelly Moran. She holds American citizenship and her ethnicity is a mix of English, Irish, Scottish, German, and Danish.

Erin Moran: Professional Life and Career

Erin Moran got an opportunity to perform in the 1966 television series ‘Daktari’ till 1969.

Also, she made her film debut form ‘How Sweet It Is!’ in 1968 and made regular appearances on ‘The Don Rickles Show’ in 1972, made a regular appearance in ‘The Courtship of Eddie’s Father’ ‘My Three Sons, ‘Bearcats!’, ‘Family Affair’, etc. She appeared in the television series ‘Gunsmoke’.

She was cast to play her best-known role in the sitcom ‘Happy Days’ in 1974, at the age of 13. Erin starred in a short-lived spin-off of the series named ‘Joanie Loves Chachi’.

She reluctantly agreed to take the role in the spin-off series and would still love to be on ‘Happy Days’ and she returned for its final season after ‘Joanie Loves Chachi’ was canceled in 1983.

Erin Moran featured in movies and had several television guest appearances such as ‘The Love Boat’, ‘Murder’, and ‘She Wrote’, and starred in the cult sci-fi horror film ‘Galaxy of Terror’ in 1981.

Erin made her appearance on VH1’s reality show ‘Celebrity Fit Club’ as a contestant in 2008 and two years later, she appeared in the independent comedy feature ‘Not Another B Movie’ in 2010.

It was reported that she would be reunited with Happy Day’s co-stars Ron Howard, Henry Winkler, and Scott Baio in the fourth season of ‘Arrested Development’ but she did not appear in the revamped Netflix series.

Awards, Nominations

Moran was awarded TV Land Awards in the category Most Wonderful Wedding for ‘Happy Days’ in 2006 and Young Artist Awards in the category Best Young Actress in a New Television Series for ‘Joanie Loves Chachi’ in 1983.

Erin Moran: Death

Erin Moran died on April 22, 2017, at the age of 56 years because of a throat cancer complication. No hard substances or illegal narcotics were used by her.

There was no illegal substance found at her home as well. She was survived by her husband Steve Fleischmann and he mentioned that her cancer increased rapidly in 2016.
