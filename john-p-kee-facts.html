Singer’s Bio

John P. Kee is a renowned American gospel singer, songwriter, and pastor. Born on June 4, 1962, in Durham, North Carolina, he was raised in a strong Christian family and started singing in his church choir at a young age. He began playing the piano at age 12 and formed his first gospel choir at the Sweet Home Baptist Church when he was 13. Kee’s love for music led him to form the New Life Community Choir, which he directed and recorded several albums with.

Career

John P. Kee started his music career at a young age. He formed his first gospel choir when he was just 13 years old and continued to pursue music while also working as a mechanic and as a police officer. He gained national recognition in 1987 with his debut album, “Wash Me,” which received much critical acclaim. Kee went on to form the New Life Community Choir, which recorded several albums, including “Show Up,” “Strength,” and “Blessed By Association.” He has released over 20 gospel albums, receiving numerous accolades over the years, including 21 Stellar Awards, two Billboard Music Awards, and a Soul Train Music Award.

In addition to his music career, John P. Kee is also the pastor of the New Life Fellowship Center in Charlotte, North Carolina. He is known for his powerful preaching and his commitment to helping others through his ministry work.
