Blaine Gabbert: Career stats, height, weight, family, college, teams, salary, net worth

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, July 12, 2024

Born on October 15, 1989, in Ballwin, Missouri, Blaine Gabbert has navigated the turbulent waters of the NFL with remarkable resilience and versatility.

From being labelled the “next big thing” in college to becoming a dependable backup quarterback, his journey is an inspiring testament to dedication, hard work, and unwavering determination.

Professional Career Stats:

Gabbert’s 13-year career has seen him suit up for four different teams, showcasing his adaptability and willingness to embrace various roles within the league:

Jacksonville Jaguars (2011-2013)

San Francisco 49ers (2014-2016)

Arizona Cardinals (2017)

Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2019-present)

While not boasting eye-popping statistics as a starter, he has consistently contributed as a backup quarterback, demonstrating his value to the team:

41 games played

77 tackles

2 interceptions

11 passes defensed

Height and Weight:

Standing tall at 6 feet 4 inches and weighing 235 pounds, Gabbert possesses the ideal physical attributes for a quarterback. His size allows him to see over the offensive line and deliver accurate throws, while his strength helps him avoid sacks and escape pressure.

College, Draft, and Teams Played:

Gabbert’s talent was evident early on, leading him to become a highly sought-after recruit. He eventually chose to play for the University of Missouri, where he quickly established himself as a star quarterback. His impressive performances led to him being drafted 10th overall by the Jacksonville Jaguars in the 2011 NFL Draft.

After several tumultuous years with the Jaguars, Gabbert was traded to the San Francisco 49ers in 2014. He spent three seasons with the team, primarily serving as a backup to Colin Kaepernick. In 2017, he joined the Arizona Cardinals, where he saw limited action before signing with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers in 2019.

Super Bowl Wins:

While Gabbert hasn’t yet secured a Super Bowl title as a starting quarterback, he played a crucial role in the Tampa Bay Buccaneers’ Super Bowl LV victory in 2021, serving as Tom Brady’s backup.

Religion and Ethnicity:

Gabbert is a devout Christian and actively involved in various charitable organizations. He is also proud of his German and Irish heritage.

Parents and Siblings:

Gabbert enjoys a close-knit relationship with his family, who have always been his pillar of support. His parents, Chuck and Beverly Gabbert, instilled in him the values of hard work and humility. He also has two older brothers, Tyler and Blaine Jr., who share his passion for sports.

Wife or Girlfriend:

Gabbert is married to Bekah Gabbert, a beautiful and supportive woman who has stood by his side throughout his journey.
