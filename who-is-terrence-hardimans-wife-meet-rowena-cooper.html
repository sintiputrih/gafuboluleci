Who is Terrence Hardimans Wife? Meet Rowena Cooper

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, September 12, 2024

Who is Terrence Hardiman’s Wife? Meet Rowena Cooper – Terrence Hardiman, an English actor born on April 6, 1937, and sadly passed away on May 9, 2023, was widely recognized for his versatile performances on stage, television, and audiobooks.

He became known for his portrayals of authoritative figures, often taking on roles such as Nazi-era personnel in series like Secret Army, Colditz, Wish Me Luck, and Enemy at the Door. In When the Boat Comes In, he portrayed a British officer.

Throughout his career, Terrence Hardiman displayed his acting prowess in various roles. He convincingly played a police inspector in acclaimed series like Prime Suspect, Juliet Bravo, and Softly, Softly. He took on the persona of a doctor in Home to Roost and The Royal, a barrister in Crown Court and The Brittas Empire, and a judge in The Bill and The Courtroom.

Terrence Hardiman’s versatility shone through as he also portrayed a twelfth-century abbot in Cadfael and an evil headmaster in the popular children’s television series The Demon Headmaster. Notably, he portrayed the prime minister Ramsay MacDonald in Richard Attenborough’s Gandhi.

In addition to his on-screen work, Hardiman made a significant impact in the world of audiobooks. He lent his voice to narrate numerous audiobooks, including series by renowned authors such as Colin Dexter, Anne Perry, and Ruth Rendell. His dedication to volunteer narration work for the non-profit Calibre Audio Library earned him a Silver Centurion service award in 2013, highlighting his commitment to sharing the joy of literature with others.

Terrence Hardiman’s acting journey began during his time at Cambridge, where he actively participated in theatrical productions for The Marlowe Society and the Cambridge University Amateur Dramatic Club. Alongside his future screen co-stars, such as Derek Jacobi, he honed his skills in both dramatic and comedic roles.

His talent and passion led him to tour with the prestigious Royal Shakespeare Company, taking on principal roles, including Starveling in Peter Brook’s landmark 1970 production of A Midsummer Night’s Dream.

Terrence Hardiman’s television work encompassed a wide range of characters and genres. Notably, he played barrister Stephen Harvesty in Granada Television’s Crown Court from 1972 to 1983. His versatility was further demonstrated when he starred as Charles Pooter in the 1979 television adaptation of George and Weedon Grossmith’s Diary of a Nobody.

One of his most memorable roles was Major Reinhardt in Secret Army, where he portrayed the head of the Luftwaffe police during the Nazi era. He also portrayed General Stuckler, a German officer, in the final series of London Weekend Television’s Wish Me Luck in 1990.

Hardiman’s talent extended to comedy as well. He delivered a keenly observed impersonation of John Le Mesurier’s Sergeant Wilson character from Dad’s Army in a 1995 episode of the time travel comedy Goodnight Sweetheart. His portrayal captured the nuances and mannerisms of the original performance, delighting viewers.

In the late 1990s, Hardiman starred as the titular character, the Demon Headmaster, in the popular children’s television drama series. The show spanned three series and resonated with both children and adults. He even made a cameo appearance in the 2019 reboot. Additionally, he portrayed Grand Wizard Egbert Hellibore in four episodes of The Worst Witch.

One of his significant recurring roles was as Father Abbot Radulfus, Brother Cadfael’s ecclesiastical superior, in the television series Cadfael. Alongside him, Michael Culver, who had previously played Major Brandt in Secret Army, portrayed Prior Robert.

Who is Terrence Hardiman’s Wife? Meet Rowena Cooper

He was married to Rowena Cooper. Rosemary Rowena Cooper was born in 1935, and she was an actress who kick-started her acting career by winning the Carlton Hobbs Bursary and joining the Radio Drama Company in 1956.

Subsequently, she joined the Dundee Repertory Theatre Company in 1959 and built an illustrious career spanning more than five decades, predominantly in British television. One of her notable roles was playing Mrs. Fenner in The Rag Trade.
