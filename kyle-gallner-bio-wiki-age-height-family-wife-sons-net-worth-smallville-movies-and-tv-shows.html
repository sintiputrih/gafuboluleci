    Kyle Gallner Actor

    Gallner proceeded to guest star on successful shows such as Judging Amy and Close to Home. He joined the cast of Veronica Mars as Cassidy Casablancas toward the end of the show’s first season, in May 2005, before becoming a season regular during the show’s second season.

    After playing Bart Allen in the Smallville episode “Run”, Gallner reprised the role on January 18, 2007, episode “Justice” and the season eight finale. He also guest-starred on Cold Case as a teenaged gunman in the episode “Rampage“, and featured in the independent film Sublime.

    In 2007, Gallner guest-starred in an episode of Law & Order: Special Victims Unit titled “Impulsive” as a high school student who accuses his teacher (played by Melissa Joan Hart) of statutory rape.

    Gallner also guest-starred in an episode of Bones as Jeremy Farrell, the suspected murderer of a child beauty pageant participant. He also played the lead role in the horror movie The Haunting in Connecticut, playing a cancer patient who is tortured by wicked spirits.

    Gallner featured in the independent film Cherry, filmed on the campuses of Kalamazoo College and Western Michigan University, along with several locations in downtown Kalamazoo, Michigan.

    Gallner plays Aaron, an Ivy-League freshman who falls for an older lady while becoming the object of affection for the woman’s young daughter. He played Quentin in the 2010 reboot of A Nightmare on Elm Street, his character is the love interest of the main character Nancy.

    Gallner played the brother of Aaron Paul’s character in the 2012 film, Smashed. He played Zach in one episode in the fourth season of AMC’s The Walking Dead. It was initially reported that he would be a recurring character, but his character was killed off, instead. In 2014, he played the role of Kurt, the antagonist to lead character Sam, in the independent film Dear White People.

    In September 2020, Gallner was cast in an undisclosed role in the fifth Scream film, which was directed by Matt Bettinelli-Olpin and Tyler Gillett. The movie is scheduled to be released on January 14, 2022.

    Kyle Gallner Nightmare on Elm Street

    A Nightmare on Elm Street is a 2010 American slasher movie directed by Samuel Bayer and written by Wesley Strick and Eric Heisserer. The movie stars Gallner, Jackie Earle Haley, Rooney Mara, Katie Cassidy, Thomas Dekker, and Kellan Lutz. Produced by Michael Bay and Platinum Dunes, it is a remake of Wes Craven’s 1984 film of the same name and a reboot of the Nightmare on Elm Street franchise.
